## Why Lawsuit Awareness Matters in iGaming Transactions

iGaming assets—online casinos, affiliate websites, domains, and other digital properties—often involve complex intellectual property, regulatory, and contractual frameworks. A pending or potential lawsuit can affect:

- **Asset valuation:** Legal disputes may decrease a property's perceived value.
- **Transaction timelines:** Ongoing litigation can delay closings or deter buyers.
- **Reputation:** Lawsuits can trigger regulatory scrutiny and impact brand credibility.
- **Risk allocation:** Parties must decide who assumes liability for existing or potential claims.

## Key Lawsuit Risks in the iGaming Asset Marketplace

Before finalizing any deal on an iGaming asset marketplace, consider these common legal risks:

### 1. Intellectual Property Infringement

Affiliate websites and online casinos rely on unique content, proprietary software, and branded domains. Disputes over copyrights, trademarks, or software licenses can lead to lawsuits, risking asset integrity and future monetization.

### 2. Regulatory Non-Compliance

The iGaming sector is governed by strict regulations, varying by region. Non-compliance with licensing or responsible gambling requirements can prompt legal action, fines, or even forced shutdowns.

### 3. Contractual Disputes

Many iGaming assets come with pre-existing agreements, such as affiliate contracts, white-label partnerships, or supplier deals. Breaches or ambiguities in these contracts can give rise to lawsuits.

## Best Practices for Mitigating Lawsuit Risk

To minimize legal exposure during iGaming M&A, both buyers and sellers should:

- **Conduct thorough due diligence:** Investigate the asset’s legal history, outstanding claims, and regulatory compliance.

- **Obtain clear representations and warranties:** Ensure contracts specify responsibility for existing or future legal issues.
