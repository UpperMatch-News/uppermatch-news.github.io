## What Is the 10 Year Treasury Yield?

The 10 year treasury yield reflects the return investors receive from holding US government bonds for a decade. It is widely regarded as a benchmark for global interest rates, influencing everything from loan costs to investment risk appetite. When yields rise, capital tends to flow out of riskier assets and into safer government bonds. Conversely, falling yields can drive investors to seek higher returns in alternative sectors—like the iGaming asset marketplace.
