### What Is a Stock Split and Why Does It Matter in iGaming?

A stock split is a corporate action where a company increases its number of shares while proportionally reducing the price per share. While stock splits are traditionally associated with publicly traded companies, the principles behind them can be translated into digital asset transactions—particularly in sectors like iGaming, where asset ownership and equity structures can be flexible and innovative.

For iGaming business acquisitions and digital gambling investments, stock split strategies can:

- **Enhance Liquidity:** By dividing a business or digital asset into smaller, more affordable shares or units, sellers can attract a broader pool of buyers on the iGaming marketplace.
- **Facilitate Partial Sales:** Owners of successful online casinos or affiliate marketing assets may choose to sell a portion of their business, retaining some equity while unlocking capital. This approach is increasingly common in the affiliate site marketplace.
- **Boost Market Visibility:** Listings that offer fractional ownership or equity splits can stand out among iGaming business listings, appealing to investors who seek diversification.
